Five steps from install to a generated settlement. Budget about five minutes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Control Center" icon="layout-panel-left">
    **Window → OmniScape** (or the toolbar button) opens the dockable **Control Center**. Everything routes through this panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place the Generator" icon="map-pin">
    Click **Place OmniScape Generator**. The actor drops into your level and links to the panel. The settlement is centred on this actor — move it and regenerate to relocate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add at least one Large asset" icon="box">
    Go to the **Build** tab and assign a **Large** building (a static mesh or Blueprint). Large is the one tier OmniScape needs — it places one landmark per node. Without it, nothing spawns.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a pattern, then Generate" icon="play">
    Choose a layout pattern (or load a [preset](/guides/presets)) on the **Layout** tab, then click **Generate**. Watch the 2D preview resolve into nodes, roads, and footprints.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save to Level" icon="save">
    Click **Save to Level** (or save the level) so the settlement survives reload and packaging.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  **The one rule:** if it looks right in the editor but vanishes in a build, you almost certainly didn't **Save to Level** after generating. [Why this matters →](/get-started/save-to-level)
</Warning>

## Explore from here

- **Randomize seed** to flip through variations — same seed always returns the same layout.
- Add **Medium** and **Small** assets for fabric and filler.
- Turn on **roads** and **walls** under [Structure](/guides/structure).
- Tune the look with the [creative-direction recipes](/guides/creative-direction).
