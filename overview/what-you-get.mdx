## The Control Center plugin

The Control Center is an editor-side panel — opened from **Window → OmniScape** or the toolbar — that manages every aspect of generation. It links to the **OmniScape Generator** actor in your level and exposes four main tabs: **Layout**, **Build**, **Structure**, and **Ambient**, plus export and bake controls.

Key capabilities surfaced by the Control Center:

- **Five layout patterns** — Radial, Voronoi, Perlin, Axial/Grid, Organic
- **Zone density and footprint controls** with volume and anchor overrides
- **Three-tier building assignment** — Large, Medium, Small; accepts any Static Mesh or Blueprint class
- **Road system** with Follow, Contour, and Level terrain-elevation modes
- **Wall system** — full and partial circuits, gates, pillars, shape styles
- **Ambient layers** — flora (Foliage Types), fauna (Blueprint actors), ambient audio
- **Landscape paint bake** for material blending at generation time
- **PCG export** to hand layout data off to external PCG graphs
- **Save to Level** — freezes generated instances as normal level actors, ready to cook

The Control Center is **editor-only** and is not included in cooked builds. See [Compatibility](/overview/compatibility) for details on what ships in packaged games.

## Twelve archetype presets

OmniScape ships with twelve named presets that cover the most common settlement archetypes. Each preset configures layout pattern, zone density, road style, wall type, and ambient density — it does **not** overwrite your mesh assignments.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Fortress & Defensive">
    Presets optimised for walled compounds, military outposts, and keep layouts. High wall density, gated entry points, compact inner zones.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Urban">
    Dense grid or radial street networks with high building coverage. Suited to city districts, market towns, and urban centres.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Rural & Village">
    Organic or Perlin layouts with lower density, wider spacing, and ambient flora weighting. Suited to hamlets, farmsteads, and roadside settlements.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Hills & Landmark">
    Layouts tuned for uneven terrain and focal-point compositions. Elevation modes and anchor usage lean heavily on natural contour following.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Presets are a starting point, not a destination. Load one to get sensible defaults, then adjust density, swap the layout pattern, or change the wall style to suit your project. Save your own preset at any time from the Control Center header.
</Tip>

<Note>
  **v3.0:** The full set of twelve presets and improved preset categories arrive in version 3.0. Version 2.7.0 ships a subset of these archetypes.
</Note>

## Blueprint & C++ API

OmniScape exposes a generation API for both Blueprint and C++ workflows.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Blueprint API" icon="diagram-project">
    Call generation, reseed, clear, and export functions from any Blueprint actor or level script. Suitable for in-game generation, runtime procedural worlds, and editor utility widgets.
  </Card>
  <Card title="C++ API" icon="code">
    The same surface is available in C++ for projects that manage generation from game modules or need tighter integration with save systems and streaming.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The API lets you change the seed, trigger generation, and read back placement data at runtime — enabling use cases like procedural world servers, roguelike map generation, and player-driven settlement growth.

## PCG Hand-off component

If your project already uses Unreal's **Procedural Content Generation (PCG)** framework, the PCG Hand-off component lets OmniScape export its layout data — zone boundaries, road splines, building positions — as PCG attribute sets that downstream PCG graphs can consume.

This is entirely optional. OmniScape does not require PCG to generate settlements; the Hand-off component is there if you want to extend or post-process the output inside your own PCG pipeline.

## Atlas wizard

<Note>
  **v3.0 feature.** The Atlas wizard is in active development and is not available in the current Fab release (2.7.0).
</Note>

The Atlas wizard, accessed from **Control Center → Layout → Atlas**, lets you load a real-world street map reference from **OpenStreetMap** data and use it to seed OmniScape's layout. The generated settlement follows the broad proportions and road topology of the reference location, giving you a grounded starting point for historically or geographically inspired environments.

## What's not included

OmniScape is a layout and instancing tool. The following are outside its scope and remain your responsibility:

- **Your art assets** — building meshes, road pieces, wall sets, foliage, fauna Blueprints, audio cues
- **Sky, lighting, and post-process** — sky sphere actors, directional lights, post-process volumes, fog
- **Materials, Nanite, and Lumen** — material assignments on your meshes, Nanite mesh settings, Lumen scene setup
- **Gameplay code** — logic inside your Blueprint actors is untouched by generation
- **Character scale** — OmniScape places at the scale of your meshes; character capsule sizing is your project's concern
