OmniScape places and instances; you direct the composition. Think in five terms: **silhouette** from above, **rhythm** from spacing, **contrast** between landmarks and filler, **grounding** from terrain, and **story** from gates, walls, and debris.

Start from an intent, then steer the dials below it.

## Recipes by intent

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Fortress & ceremonial core" icon="castle">
    **Goal:** a dominant silhouette — there is a keep and a ring.
    - **Layout:** Radial / concentric, central district + outer wards, modest radial jitter, aligned gate spokes.
    - **Build:** Cluster, strong **Large** tier, low **Edge Density** so the core stays legible.
    - **Roads:** Curved or natural; Follow on uneven ground; width matched to gates.
    - **Walls:** On · perimeter fit toward the hull · Angular · full loop · gates Auto or Anchors.
    - **Environment:** Sparse vegetation outside, rocks at the base, light debris inside for life.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Planned city & urban grid" icon="grid-3x3">
    **Goal:** streets look designed; façades align.
    - **Layout:** Voronoi for organic blocks, or Axial with **Parallel grid** for true blocks.
    - **Build:** Roadside · wider plot frontage · orient medium/small to road.
    - **Roads:** Straight · stable width · intersection mesh + radius scale for clean hubs.
    - **Walls:** Usually off for modern; on for old town (Natural shape, blended fit).
    - **Watch:** Roadside ignores per-anchor medium/small lists; expect longer generates at high node counts.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ridge & mountain village" icon="mountain">
    **Goal:** the settlement clings to slopes, not a flat disc pasted on terrain.
    - **Layout:** Perlin / noise-driven; tune feature size and roughness for several small ridge clusters.
    - **Build:** Cluster often reads better than roadside on steep ground.
    - **Roads:** Follow · natural or curved · narrower width for paths.
    - **Environment:** Force upright on trees; rocks near buildings; min distance so canopies don't eat roofs.
    - **Watch:** Level elevation on hilly ground floats road meshes — use Follow or Contour.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Rural county & scatter" icon="tractor">
    **Goal:** loose rhythm — yards, side spurs, lived-in edges.
    - **Layout:** Organic cluster · higher min node distance for spread.
    - **Build:** Roadside · varied plots · spur slots + driveway for farmsteads.
    - **Roads:** Natural · Follow · road redundancy up for loops.
    - **Walls:** Off or partial fence-line.
    - **Environment:** Rich vegetation; debris layers (Kind = Debris) clustering near homes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ruins & decay" icon="building-2">
    **Goal:** interruption and negative space — broken enclosure, nature reclaiming.
    - **Layout:** Organic or Perlin · low node count.
    - **Build:** Lower per-node counts · mixed weights for uneven skylines.
    - **Walls:** On · **partial coverage** · Organic shape · pillars spaced, not only corners.
    - **Environment:** Heavy debris · rocks in paths · max distance widened so vegetation encroaches.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Oasis & vignette" icon="tent">
    **Goal:** one contained shot — a camp, caravanserai, or stop.
    - **World:** An **inclusion volume** — a small closed loop staging the scene.
    - **Layout:** Radial or organic inside the volume · 1–3 nodes.
    - **Build:** Cluster · Large = hero tent or inn · optional anchors.
    - **Environment:** Rocks and sparse veg at the edge; debris at the core.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Starting bands

| Situation | Radius (cm) | Nodes | Edge density |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single landmark / oasis | 1,500 – 5,000 | 1 – 3 | 0 – 0.25 |
| Hamlet | 4,000 – 9,000 | 2 – 5 | 0.1 – 0.35 |
| Village / town | 8,000 – 18,000 | 5 – 15 | 0.35 – 0.65 |
| City / sprawl | 15,000 – 35,000+ | 12 – 40 | 0.5 – 1.0 |
| Fortified core | any | 1 – 6 | 0 – 0.35 |

## Realism checklist

- **One scale language** — keep radius, road width, and plots in consistent centimetres against a human-scale reference.
- **Let the ground show** — on hilly sites, set buildings to sit into the slope; drop a foundation mesh to ground them.
- **Pivot honesty** — fix offset and bounds before blaming placement.
- **Strategic walls** — assign a tower/bastion mesh and a sensible tower count for a fortified read.
- **Lock the seed** when the composition works, then iterate materials in Unreal.

  OmniScape composes space; it doesn't replace your sky, lighting, post, fog, or material choices. Spatial composition here — cinematic finish in Unreal's lookdev stack.
