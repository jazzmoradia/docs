Volumes and anchors let you direct the layout by hand without leaving the procedural workflow.

## Spline volumes

Place an **OmniScape Volume** actor, shape its closed spline, and set its mode.

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Exclusion" icon="ban">
    No spawn inside — carve out water, cliffs, or a hand-built set-piece.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Inclusion" icon="square-dashed">
    Spawn only inside — stage a camp, a quarry floor, or a contained vignette.
  </Card>
</Columns>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Place and shape">
    Drop an **OmniScape Volume** and close the spline around the region.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the mode">
    Choose **Exclusion** or **Inclusion**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign to the Generator">
    Add it to **Spline volumes** on the Generator, then generate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  A volume that isn't assigned to the Generator's list does nothing. Always add it before you generate.
</Warning>

## Anchors

Place **OmniScape Anchor** actors to fix layout nodes. The pattern fills the rest up to **Num Nodes**.

- **Landmark** — an optional mesh or Blueprint that drives that node's **Large** building.
- **Influence** — how strongly the anchor shapes nearby placement.
- **Per-anchor lists** — optional medium/small building lists for that node.
- **Zone override** — set the node's zone directly; a per-anchor override always wins.

Anchors can also drive **wall gates** when the gate mode allows.

<Note>
  Per-anchor medium/small lists apply in **Cluster** mode only. **Roadside** placement uses the global medium/small lists. A **Landmark** on an anchor still drives that node's Large building in either mode.
</Note>
