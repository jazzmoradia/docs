Roads give your settlement its skeleton — they determine how traffic flows, how districts connect, and how the ground reads from above. Walls give it presence and boundary, turning a scatter of buildings into a place with an inside and an outside. Together they're the two most visible structural systems in OmniScape, and getting them right shapes everything else.

## Roads

### Enabling roads

Open the **Structure** tab on your OmniScape generator and toggle **Generate Roads** on. The road network generates from the node graph, connecting settlements according to your style and terrain settings.

### Road styles

The style controls the horizontal character of the road path.

| Style | Character |
|-------|-----------|
| **Straight** | Planned, direct routes — good for grids, military roads, and formal urban layouts |
| **Curved** | Sweeping arcs that feel designed — town boulevards, estate approaches |
| **Natural** | Worn, organic paths that follow the landscape — villages, trails, rural routes |

<Tip>
Pair **Natural** with **Contour** terrain mode on hilly sites for roads that read as genuinely worn into the hillside.
</Tip>

### Terrain modes

Terrain mode controls how the road responds vertically to the landscape beneath it.

| Mode | Behaviour | Best for |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Follow** | Traces the terrain surface, including hills and dips | Hilly or varied ground — the default safe choice |
| **Contour** (v3) | Caps the maximum grade on steep slopes via switchbacks and level pads | Steep sites where Follow produces too-sharp climbs |
| **Level** | Flattens each road segment to a single elevation | Genuinely flat ground only |

<Warning>
**Level roads float on hills.** If your settlement sits on any meaningful slope, Level mode will produce roads that hover above the terrain surface. Use **Follow** or **Contour** instead. On steep sites, **Contour + Natural** style is a reliable combination.
</Warning>

Advanced road tuning exposes additional controls: cut & fill depth, sample spacing, and smoothing pass count. These live under the **Advanced** section of the Road tab and are worth revisiting once your basic layout is locked.

<Note>
**Legacy mode** is also available under Advanced. It applies the older road generation rules used in pre-v3 seeds. Only enable it when working with a saved settlement that was generated on an older version of OmniScape — it ensures the road network reproduces identically from the original seed.
</Note>

### Road width

OmniScape separates **clearance** (the space the road reserves in the layout) from **visual width** (how wide the mesh actually looks). They are independent — changing the visual width scale or tiling does not affect building placement or road clearance.

<Tip>
If buildings are crowding the road, increase **Width** (clearance). If you just want the mesh to look wider or narrower, adjust **Width Scale** and **Tiling** without touching clearance.
</Tip>

**Width bands for art direction:**

| Read | Band (cm) |
|------|-----------|
| Footpath / trail | 80 — 250 |
| Town street | 300 — 700 |
| Main road / boulevard | 700 — 1,400 |

### Road assets

Rather than a single mesh repeated along every road, OmniScape supports a **weighted road asset list** — multiple meshes with relative weights, giving you variation across the network. Assign your assets in the Road Assets list and adjust weights to control how often each appears.

Blueprint entries in the asset list can spawn actors at spline points when **Blueprint Spawn Mode** is set to anything other than None. Use this for props, Shader World brushes, or other spline-driven actors.

<Note>
Single road mesh mode still works for setups that don't need variation — it's not deprecated, just less flexible.
</Note>

### Intersection meshes

By default, OmniScape trims road ends cleanly at junctions. To create deliberate hub pads at intersections:

1. Assign an **Intersection Mesh** in the Road settings.
2. Adjust **Radius Scale** to size the pad relative to the roads meeting at that junction.

This works well for market squares, toll gates, or any junction you want to read as a landmark.

## Walls

### Enabling walls

Toggle **Generate Walls** on in the **Structure** tab. The wall ring is generated around the settlement footprint according to your perimeter, shape, and coverage settings.

### Perimeter fit

**Perimeter Fit** slides between two extremes:

| Value | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **0** | Wall hugs the building footprint tightly |
| **1** | Wall sits at the full settlement radius — a grand enclosing ring |

Values in between give you controlled setback. Use a low value for dense walled towns; use a high value for keeps or fortresses where the wall ring is part of the composition.

### Shape styles

| Style | Character |
|-------|-----------|
| **Angular** | Crisp, geometric — fortifications, planned enclosures |
| **Natural** | Light variation, readable silhouette — the default for most settlements |
| **Organic** | Weathered, irregular — ruins, ancient walls, grown-over enclosures |

**Control point count** (8–96, default 36) sets the polygon density of the wall ring. Lower values give a chunky, faceted silhouette; higher values smooth it out.

### Wall parameter reference

| Parameter | Range | Default |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| Perimeter fit | 0 — 1 | 1 |
| Control point count | 8 — 96 | 36 |
| Coverage | 0.05 — 1.0 | 0.75 |
| Height (cm) | ≥ 50 | 400 |
| Thickness (cm) | ≥ 10 | 200 |
| Tiling | 0.01 — 20 | 1 |
| Pillar spacing | 0.001 — 0.5 | 0.01 |

### Full vs partial coverage

- **Full** closes the wall ring into a complete loop.
- **Partial** breaks the ring — useful for ruins, incomplete construction, or settlements that are only partially enclosed. Coverage (0.05–1.0, default 0.75) controls what fraction of the perimeter is filled.

<Warning>
**Full perimeter requires a closable layout.** If your node arrangement or road network prevents a clean closure, OmniScape may produce a broken ring even with Full selected. If that happens, switch to **Partial** or adjust your nodes and roads until the layout can close cleanly.
</Warning>

### Gates

| Mode | Behaviour |
|------|-----------|
| **Auto** | OmniScape places gates at road intersections with the wall |
| **Anchors** | Gates appear at anchor positions where the wall crosses |
| **Evenly spaced** | Fixed gate count (0–32) distributed around the perimeter |

### Pillars

Pillars can appear at **corners only** for a clean, restrained look, or **spaced along the wall** for a rhythmic defensive silhouette. Assign pillar meshes in the Pillars weighted list alongside your wall segment meshes.

### Optional v3 wall assets

<Accordion title="Tower / bastion mesh and foundation / plinth mesh">
These assets are optional and not required for the standard wall look.

- **Tower / bastion mesh + count** — snaps tower actors to the sharpest corners of the wall ring first, then distributes remaining count around the perimeter.
- **Foundation / plinth mesh** — places a base mesh under buildings on sloped ground, hiding the gap between building and terrain.
- **Per-class road meshes** (Arterial / Collector / Local) — lets you assign different meshes to road segments by their classification in the hierarchy.

None of these are required. Add them when you want extra fidelity or when your art direction calls for distinct corner towers.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
Use **Rebuild Walls Only** (when available from the generator actions) to iterate on wall settings — shape, coverage, gates, pillar placement — without triggering a full settlement regeneration. This makes wall art direction significantly faster.
</Tip>

## Landscape Bake (Beta)

OmniScape can optionally paint additive landscape layer weights based on the generated layout — roads, walls, building pads, and the settlement boundary all contribute to named paint layers.

<Note>
Landscape bake is a **Beta feature**. Enable Beta mode in the status bar (bottom-right of the editor) before running it. The bake runs as a **separate editor action after generation** — it does not happen automatically when you click Generate.
</Note>

This is useful for driving material blends (dirt paths, cobblestone, cleared ground) directly from the layout without manual painting.
