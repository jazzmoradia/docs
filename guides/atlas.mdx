Atlas bases a settlement on a real location. Open it from the **fold-corner** on the preview canvas (**Control Center → Layout**), and work through three steps.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate" icon="search">
    Type a city, neighbourhood, or landmark name — or paste a map link — and **Import This Location**. Offline, import an `.osm` or `.geojson` file, or a **screenshot**: a colour- and edge-aware trace converts a map image into usable road geometry.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tune" icon="sliders">
    Choose how closely generation follows the reference, and at what scale (below).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate" icon="play">
    **Generate Settlement** — your buildings, walls, and ambient layers compose on the imported skeleton.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Fidelity

| Level | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Inspired** | Real-world shape as a loose influence; full procedural freedom. |
| **Guided** | Blends imported roads with procedural infill inside the coverage area. |
| **Faithful** | Stays as close to the source geometry as possible. |

## Import scale & quality

- **Import Scale** brings data in at **Real World** size or automatically **Fit to Settlement**.
- Every import reports a clear **Ready / Limited / Failed** status. Failed imports are refused with an actionable message; screenshot imports always produce at least a usable result.

<Warning>
  OSM data requires attribution — **© OpenStreetMap contributors** — shown after import. Keep it when you publish imagery derived from real data.
</Warning>

<Note>
  Use **Control Center → Layout → Atlas** for the creative workflow. The actor's **Details → Atlas** holds diagnostics and advanced import settings only.
</Note>
