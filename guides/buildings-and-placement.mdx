OmniScape doesn't ship with opinionated architecture — it ships with a placement engine. You bring your own Static Meshes and Blueprints; OmniScape figures out where they go. The Build tab is where that handoff happens: you assign your art to three tiers, tell OmniScape how often each piece should appear, and choose whether buildings cluster around landmark nodes or line up along roads. The generator handles the rest — sampling positions, respecting footprint clearance, obeying slope limits, and orienting buildings to face the nearest street.

---

## Building Tiers

OmniScape sorts buildings into three tiers, each playing a distinct visual role in the settlement.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Large" icon="landmark">
    **One per node.** Landmark structures that define the silhouette — keeps, cathedrals, market halls, guild houses. These anchor every node and are always placed before Medium or Small.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Medium" icon="building">
    **Main fabric.** The buildings that fill the settlement between landmarks — houses, workshops, inns, stables. These form the majority of what the player sees at street level.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Small" icon="store">
    **Edge noise.** Filler structures at the margins — sheds, outhouses, lean-tos, stalls, crates. Small buildings break up repetition and soften the perimeter of clusters.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The three tiers together create the visual hierarchy that makes a settlement read correctly at every distance: Large for the skyline read, Medium for the street read, Small for the detail read.

<Warning>
  **Nothing generates if no Large asset is assigned.** OmniScape requires at least one Large-tier entry before it will place any buildings. Add a placeholder mesh to the Large tier first, then fill in Medium and Small.
</Warning>

## Adding Assets

Each tier holds a list of **slots**. Each slot represents one building option that OmniScape can choose from when filling that tier.

### Static Mesh vs Blueprint

| Asset type | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Static Mesh** | Geometry-only buildings — no runtime logic needed. OmniScape instances these for performance. |
| **Blueprint** | Buildings that require logic: doors that open, lights that toggle, interactable props. Spawned as full Actors. |

If you set both a Static Mesh and a Blueprint on the same slot, **the Blueprint takes priority** — the mesh is ignored.

### Bounds Override

OmniScape reads each asset's bounding box to avoid footprint overlaps. Some kits ship with incorrect pivots or bounds that don't match the visible geometry — a tower mesh whose pivot is at a corner rather than the base, for example.

Use **Bounds Override** on the slot to supply a corrected bounding box manually.

<Tip>
  If buildings are floating above the ground or clipping into it, the first thing to check is the mesh pivot and bounds. A Bounds Override fix takes seconds and is far faster than modifying the source asset.
</Tip>

## Cluster vs Roadside Placement

How OmniScape distributes Medium and Small buildings around each node is controlled by the placement mode.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Cluster (default)">
    **Cluster** samples positions in rings around each node. Buildings radiate outward from the landmark, forming the dense cores typical of organic settlements.

    - Works with **per-anchor Medium/Small overrides** — you can pin specific building lists to individual anchors.
    - Ring density and radius are influenced by Num Nodes, Settlement Radius, and the per-tier Max Per Node setting.
    - Best for: organic layouts (Voronoi, Perlin, Organic), fortified settlements where buildings pack around a keep.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Roadside">
    **Roadside** places buildings as plots along roads, using frontage (how close the building face is to the road), depth (plot length), and variation (stagger and offset) to create a street of properties.

    - Creates a much more urban, street-facing character than Cluster.
    - **Does not respect per-anchor Medium/Small overrides** — those are Cluster-only. Large landmarks on anchors still apply.
    - Best for: Axial / Grid settlements, market streets, colonial towns, anywhere roads should read as lined with buildings.

    <Note>
      If you switch to Roadside and your per-anchor building lists seem to be ignored, that's expected. Roadside uses the global Medium and Small lists only. Move specialised buildings to the Large tier and anchor them, or switch back to Cluster for anchor-driven placement.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Weights

Each slot in a tier has a **Weight** value. OmniScape uses these as relative odds when selecting which building to place — it does not treat them as absolute counts.

| Weight A | Weight B | Effective odds |
|---|---|---|
| 10 | 10 | 50 / 50 |
| 10 | 1 | ~91% A, ~9% B |
| 5 | 5 | 50 / 50 |
| 1 | 0 | A only (B never placed) |

Only the **ratio** between weights matters. Setting all weights to 10 is identical to setting them all to 1. Use high-weight values when you want a building to dominate and low-weight values for rare accent pieces.

## Building Counts Per Node

The number of Medium and Small buildings generated around each node is controlled by the **Max Per Node** settings on each tier. Use this rule of thumb to calibrate the density feel you want:

| Density feel | Medium / node | Small / node |
|---|---|---|
| Sparse | 2 — 6 | 4 — 12 |
| Typical | 6 — 12 | 12 — 24 |
| Busy | 12 — 30 | 24 — 80 |

These are starting points, not rules. A settlement on difficult terrain with high Max Building Slope rejection may feel sparser than the numbers suggest. Adjust upward to compensate.

## Scale and Facing

### Per-Tier Scale

Each tier has a **Scale** multiplier that applies uniformly to all assets in that tier. This is a visual-only adjustment — it does not affect footprint clearance calculations, which always use the original bounds.

Use Scale to push Large landmarks to feel truly landmark-sized relative to Medium and Small, or to shrink Small buildings to feel suitably improvised and rough.

### Face Toward Road

Enable **Face Toward Road** per tier to orient buildings so their designated front faces the nearest road. Disable it for buildings that should present a courtyard or random orientation, such as market stalls or scattered outbuildings.

| Facing on | Facing off |
|---|---|
| Street-fronting houses, shops, gates | Barns, outhouses, rubble, ruins, courtyard buildings |

## Spur Streets

A **Spur Street** is a short dead-end road that branches off a main road — functionally a driveway. When you assign a Spur Street to a slot, OmniScape generates the spur, then places the associated building at the end of it.

Pairing a Spur with a yard-style Medium building and a gate or fence Small building creates a convincing farmstead or manor read: road → spur → gate → yard → main house.

<Tip>
  Spurs work best with Cluster placement and low Road Redundancy. Too many spurs on a dense road network can produce overlapping dead-ends — use Max Per Node on the spur-assigned tier to keep them sparse.
</Tip>

## Sprawl (Edge Density)

**Sprawl** fills the gaps between main building clusters and roads with additional Small buildings, without requiring you to increase Num Nodes or Max Per Node across the whole settlement.

Find it at: **Build → Sprawl → Edge Density** (parameter name: `SprawlEdgeDensity`)

| Value | Effect |
|---|---|
| 0 (default) | Sprawl off — gaps remain empty |
| 0.1 — 0.4 | Light scatter — a few outliers at the edges |
| 0.5 — 0.8 | Moderate sprawl — settlement feels like it has grown beyond its planned core |
| 0.9 — 1.0 | Heavy sprawl — dense fill; combines with high Max Per Node to create very busy settlements |

<Note>
  Edge Density is the current artist-facing sprawl control. The legacy **Settlement Density** field still exists for backwards compatibility but does not drive the same system. Use Edge Density for new settlements.
</Note>

Edge Density is particularly effective when combined with a low Num Nodes + large Settlement Radius setup — it fills the otherwise-empty outer rings with scattered small buildings that imply organic growth beyond the settlement core.

## Common Pitfalls

<Accordion title="Nothing generates — settlement is empty after Generate">
  OmniScape requires at least one Large-tier asset before placing any buildings. Open **Build → Large** and add at least one mesh or Blueprint slot. Medium and Small are optional, but Large is required.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Roadside buildings ignore my per-anchor lists">
  Per-anchor Medium and Small overrides only apply to **Cluster** placement. Roadside reads the global Medium and Small tier lists. Either switch to Cluster, or promote anchor-specific buildings to Large tier and assign them via anchor overrides there.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Buildings are floating or buried">
  The mesh pivot or bounds don't match the visible geometry. Use **Bounds Override** on the affected slot to supply corrected bounds. Also check **Max Building Slope** — if the terrain under a building shifted during landscape editing, the building may no longer be considered valid and could re-place oddly on regenerate.
</Accordion>
